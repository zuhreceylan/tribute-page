Hedy Lamarr

Hedy Lamarr was an Austrian-American actress and self-taught inventor. While she was a famous Hollywood star during the Golden Age of cinema, she also co-invented a frequency-hopping spread spectrum technology. This invention later became the foundation for modern wireless communication such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and GPS.

Here's a timeline of Hedy Lamarr's life:

  • 1914 – Born in Vienna, Austria-Hungary as Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler
  • 1933 – Gains international attention for her role in the film Ecstasy, which was considered controversial at the time
  • 1937 – Leaves Austria and moves to Hollywood to pursue an acting career
  • 1938 – Signs a contract with MGM and becomes known as “the most beautiful woman in the world”
  • 1940 – Becomes a major Hollywood star, appearing in films such as Algiers and Boom Town
  • 1941 – Co-invents a frequency-hopping communication system with composer George Antheil to prevent enemy interception of torpedo signals
  • 1942 – Receives a patent for the frequency-hopping spread spectrum technology
  • 1950s – Continues acting while independently working on various inventions in her spare time
  • 1960s – Her invention begins to be recognized for its potential military and communication applications
  • 1997 – Receives the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award for her contributions to technology
  • 2014 – Inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame
  • 2000 – Dies at the age of 85 in Orlando, Florida
